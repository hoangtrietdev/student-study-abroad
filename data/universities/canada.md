## 1. University of Toronto
- **QS World Rank**: #25
- **Location**: Toronto, Ontario
- **Type**: Public Research University (U15 Group)
- **Tuition (Undergrad — International)**: CAD $45,000–$68,000/year
- **Tuition (Postgrad — International)**: CAD $20,000–$35,000/year
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~43%
- **Popular Programs**: Computer Science, Engineering, Medicine, Business (Rotman), Law
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 6.5–7.0+ / TOEFL 100+
- **Scholarships**:
  - Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship (full funding: tuition + living, 4 years)
  - University of Toronto Scholar Awards ($7,500–$11,000/year)
  - Ontario Graduate Scholarship
- **Notable**: Lester B. Pearson Scholarship is one of Canada's most prestigious — fully funded for undergrad. Strong startup ecosystem.
- **Website**: https://www.utoronto.ca

## General Notes for Canada
- **Visa**: Canadian Study Permit. Processing: 2–12 weeks (apply early via IRCC).
- **Cost of Living**: Vancouver/Toronto: CAD $18,000–$28,000/year; Other cities: CAD $12,000–$18,000/year
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week on campus (off-campus with Study Permit). Full-time during holidays.
- **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)**: 1–3 years depending on program length. Pathway to Permanent Residence.
- **Healthcare**: International students in most provinces must purchase private health insurance (~CAD $600–$900/year).
- **Application**: Direct to university (no central system). Deadlines vary Nov–Feb.
