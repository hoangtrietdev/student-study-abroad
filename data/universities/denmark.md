## 1. University of Copenhagen (UCPH)
- **QS World Rank**: #97
- **Location**: Copenhagen
- **Type**: Public Research University
- **Tuition (EU/EEA students)**: FREE
- **Tuition (Non-EU International)**: DKK 50,000–120,000/year (~€6,700–€16,100/year)
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~25%
- **Popular Programs**: Medicine, Pharmaceutical Sciences, Law, Natural Sciences, Social Sciences
- **Language Requirements**: Danish C1 (Danish programs); IELTS 6.5+ / TOEFL 88+ (English programs)
- **Scholarships**:
  - University of Copenhagen Scholarship for non-EU students (tuition waiver)
  - Danish Government Scholarships (Quota 1 — for specific nationalities)
  - Nordic Excellence Scholarship
- **Notable**: Denmark's largest and most prestigious university. 9 Nobel laureates affiliated. World-class research in medicine and natural sciences.
- **Website**: https://www.ku.dk/english

## 2. Technical University of Denmark (DTU)
- **QS World Rank**: #146
- **Location**: Kongens Lyngby (near Copenhagen)
- **Type**: Public Technical University
- **Tuition (EU/EEA)**: FREE
- **Tuition (Non-EU)**: DKK 75,000–130,000/year (~€10,000–€17,500/year)
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~35%
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering, Food Science, Aquatic Science, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 6.5+ / TOEFL 88+ (English-taught programs available)
- **Scholarships**:
  - DTU Scholarship for Non-EU Students (tuition + DKK 7,000/month living allowance)
  - Danish Energy Agency Research Scholarships
  - Novo Nordisk Foundation Scholarships
- **Notable**: Denmark's leading technical university. Strong maritime, food, and renewable energy engineering. Many programs available in English.
- **Website**: https://www.dtu.dk/english

## General Notes for Denmark
- **Visa**: Student Residence Permit. EU/EEA citizens need only register.
- **Cost of Living**: Copenhagen: DKK 10,000–14,000/month (~€1,340–€1,880/month). Very expensive.
- **SU (State Education Support)**: Danish citizens and EU students residing in Denmark may receive DKK 6,321/month — a major benefit.
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week during semester on student residence permit.
- **Language**: Danish used in most public life, but English is widely spoken (Danes have among world's highest English proficiency).
- **Application**: Via the individual university portals. Deadlines: March 1–15 for autumn start.
- **EU advantage**: EU/EEA students study FREE in Denmark. Major financial benefit.
