## 1. Technical University of Munich (TUM)
- **QS World Rank**: #37
- **Location**: Munich, Bavaria
- **Type**: Public Technical University (Excellence Initiative)
- **Tuition (All students)**: ~€150/semester admin fee — effectively FREE
- **Tuition (Postgrad)**: ~€150/semester admin fee — effectively FREE
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~8% for international master's (highly competitive)
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering, Computer Science, Life Sciences, Management, Physics
- **Language Requirements**: German B2–C1 (German programs); IELTS 6.5–7.0+ (English master's)
- **Scholarships**:
  - DAAD Scholarship (German Academic Exchange, full funding available)
  - Deutschlandstipendium (€300/month, merit-based)
  - TUM Graduate School Fellowships
  - Bavarian State Scholarship
- **Notable**: Germany's #1 ranked university. Extremely strong in engineering and entrepreneurship. Located in Munich — Germany's economic powerhouse.
- **Website**: https://www.tum.de

## General Notes for Germany
- **Visa**: National Visa (Study) — Type D. Processing: 4–12 weeks.
- **Cost of Living**: Munich/Frankfurt: €1,000–€1,400/month; Other cities: €700–€1,000/month
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 120 full days or 240 half-days per year on student visa.
- **Tuition**: Most public German universities charge ONLY semester fees (€150–€400), not tuition — one of the world's best deals for quality education.
- **Language**: German essential for daily life even if studying in English. A1–B1 recommended before arrival.
- **DAAD Scholarship**: German government scholarship — among the most generous in the world, open to all nationalities.
- **Post-Study**: 18-month job-seeking visa after graduation to find work in Germany.
- **Application**: Via uni-assist portal for many universities. Deadlines: May 15 (winter) / Nov 15 (summer).
