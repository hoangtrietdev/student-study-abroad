## 1. Trinity College Dublin (TCD)
- **QS World Rank**: #82
- **Location**: Dublin 2
- **Type**: Public Research University
- **Tuition (EU/EEA)**: ~€3,000–€6,000/year
- **Tuition (Non-EU International)**: €18,000–€48,000/year
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~25%
- **Popular Programs**: Computer Science, Law, Medicine, Business, Engineering
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 6.5–7.0+ / TOEFL 88–100+
- **Scholarships**:
  - Trinity College Dublin Global Excellence Scholarship (25%–100% tuition waiver)
  - Government of Ireland Postgraduate Scholarship Programme
  - Book of Kells Scholarship (merit-based)
- **Notable**: Ireland's #1 and oldest university (est. 1592). Home to the Book of Kells. Strong fintech and computer science programs. Dublin is EU tech hub (Google, Meta, Apple EMEA HQ).
- **Website**: https://www.tcd.ie

## General Notes for Ireland
- **Visa**: Study Visa (Single Entry or Multiple Entry). Non-EEA students must register with GNIB/IRP. Processing: 4–8 weeks.
- **Cost of Living**: Dublin: €1,200–€1,700/month; Other cities: €900–€1,300/month. Student accommodation very competitive in Dublin.
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week during term; 40 hrs/week during holidays on study visa.
- **Post-Study Work**: Third Level Graduate Programme — stay 12–24 months after graduation to find work. Excellent pathway to EU permanent residency.
- **Tech Ecosystem**: Dublin hosts EMEA HQ of Google, Meta, Apple, LinkedIn, Salesforce, Twitter/X — excellent internship/job opportunities.
- **English Language**: Only English-speaking country in the EU after Brexit — advantage for international students wanting to work in EU.
- **Application**: Via CAO for undergrad; direct to universities for postgrad. CAO deadline: Feb 1.
