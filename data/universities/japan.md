## 1. University of Tokyo (東京大学 / Todai)
- **QS World Rank**: #32
- **Location**: Bunkyō, Tokyo
- **Type**: National Public Research University
- **Tuition (International)**: ¥535,800/year (~$3,600/year) — same as Japanese students
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~30% for international programs
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering, Medicine, Law, Economics, Science
- **Language Requirements**: Japanese N2+ (Japanese programs); IELTS 6.5+ (English programs — PEAK, GSGC)
- **Scholarships**:
  - MEXT Scholarship (Japanese Government — full funding: tuition + ¥117,000–¥145,000/month + flights)
  - JASSO Scholarship (¥48,000/month for international students)
  - UTokyo Scholarship for International Students
  - Monbukagakusho Research Student Scholarship
- **Notable**: Japan's most prestigious university. Known as Japan's Harvard. Strong in all fields. PEAK (Programs in English at Komaba) offers English-medium undergraduate.
- **Website**: https://www.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en

## General Notes for Japan
- **Visa**: Student Visa (留学ビザ). Apply at Japanese Embassy. Processing: 2–3 months (Certificate of Eligibility first).
- **Cost of Living**: Tokyo: ¥120,000–180,000/month (~$800–$1,200); Osaka/Kyoto: ~¥100,000–150,000/month; Sapporo/Sendai/Fukuoka: ~¥80,000–120,000/month
- **MEXT Scholarship**: Japan's prestigious government scholarship — covers full tuition + monthly living allowance (¥117,000–¥145,000) + flights. Apply via Embassy or University recommendation. HIGHLY RECOMMENDED.
- **Language**: JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) N2 or higher strongly recommended even for English programs. N5–N4 helpful for daily life.
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 28 hrs/week with a work permit on student visa.
- **Japanese Yen Note**: Japan's low tuition is partly because the yen has weakened — national university tuition is remarkably affordable.
- **Application**: Direct to university. For MEXT: Embassy route (spring) or University recommendation route.
