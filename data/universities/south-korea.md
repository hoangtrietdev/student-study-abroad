# Top Universities in South Korea

## 1. Seoul National University (SNU / 서울대학교)
- **QS World Rank**: #31
- **Location**: Gwanak-gu, Seoul
- **Type**: Public National Research University
- **Tuition (International)**: KRW 4,000,000–7,000,000/semester (~$3,000–$5,300/year)
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~15–20%
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering, Medicine, Law, Business (GSB), Liberal Arts
- **Language Requirements**: TOPIK Level 3+ (Korean programs) / IELTS 6.5+ (English programs)
- **Scholarships**:
  - Korean Government Scholarship (KGSP / GKS) — full funding: tuition + KRW 900,000/month + flights + Korean language training
  - SNU Global Leader Award (50%–100% tuition for undergrad)
  - Gukje (International) Scholarship
- **Notable**: South Korea's most prestigious university — equivalent of Korea's Harvard/Oxford. Strong government & conglomerate connections (Samsung, LG, Hyundai alumni).
- **Website**: https://www.useoul.edu (English) / https://www.snu.ac.kr

## 4. KAIST (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology)
- **QS World Rank**: #63
- **Location**: Yuseong-gu, Daejeon (+ Seoul campus)
- **Type**: Public National Science & Technology University
- **Tuition (International)**: KRW 2,000,000–4,000,000/semester — but most students receive fellowships
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~20% for international master's
- **Popular Programs**: Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, AI, Robotics, Mathematics
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 6.5+ / TOEFL 85+ (most programs in English for grad)
- **Scholarships**:
  - KAIST Full-Ride Scholarship (most graduate students: full tuition + KRW 300,000–500,000/month)
  - KGSP Scholarship
  - KAIST Presidential Fellowship
- **Notable**: South Korea's MIT equivalent. Government-funded technology powerhouse. Most PhD students fully funded. Extremely competitive admissions.
- **Website**: https://www.kaist.ac.kr/en

## General Notes for South Korea
- **Visa**: D-2 Student Visa. Processing: 2–4 weeks at Korean Embassy.
- **Cost of Living**: Seoul: KRW 800,000–1,500,000/month (~$600–$1,130); Other cities cheaper by 30–40%.
- **KGSP (Korean Government Scholarship Program)**: One of Asia's best fully-funded scholarships — covers tuition, accommodation, living allowance, flights, and Korean language training. HIGHLY RECOMMENDED.
- **Korean Language**: TOPIK required for Korean-medium programs. Many universities offer one-year Korean language training for scholarship recipients.
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week on D-2 visa (with employment permit).
- **K-Culture**: South Korea is safe, technologically advanced, and culturally rich. Korean Wave (Hallyu) has made it very popular for international students.
- **Application**: Direct to universities. KGSP applications open September–November for following academic year.
