## 1. KTH Royal Institute of Technology
- **QS World Rank**: #98
- **Location**: Stockholm
- **Type**: Public Technical University
- **Tuition (EU/EEA)**: FREE
- **Tuition (Non-EU)**: SEK 150,000–295,000/year (~€13,000–€26,000/year)
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~25%
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering Physics, Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Industrial Design, Architecture
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 6.5+ / TOEFL 90+ (most programs in English)
- **Scholarships**:
  - KTH Scholarship (75%–100% tuition waiver for non-EU)
  - Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (SI) — fully funded
  - Sweden-based industry scholarships (Ericsson, Spotify, Volvo)
- **Notable**: Sweden's leading technical university. Strong partnerships with Ericsson, Spotify, Klarna. Located in Stockholm — tech hub of Scandinavia.
- **Website**: https://www.kth.se/en

## General Notes for Sweden
- **Visa**: Swedish Residence Permit for Studies. EU/EEA need only register.
- **Cost of Living**: Stockholm: SEK 12,000–16,000/month (~€1,040–€1,390/month); Other cities: SEK 8,000–12,000/month
- **Swedish Institute (SI) Scholarship**: HIGHLY RECOMMENDED — fully covers tuition + living allowance (~€10,000 total/year) for selected nationalities. Very competitive.
- **Part-time Work**: No restrictions on work hours for residence permit holders during studies.
- **Language**: English widely spoken (EF English Proficiency Index — Sweden ranked among top 5 globally).
- **Application**: Via Universityadmissions.se (centralized portal). Deadlines: January 15 (autumn start).
- **EU advantage**: EU/EEA students study FREE in Sweden. Major cost benefit.
