## 1. National Taiwan University (NTU / 國立臺灣大學)
- **QS World Rank**: #69
- **Location**: Da'an District, Taipei
- **Type**: National Research University
- **Tuition (International)**: NTD 23,000–50,000/semester (~$720–$1,570/semester) — very affordable
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~30% for international programs
- **Popular Programs**: Engineering, Computer Science, Medicine, Business, Social Sciences
- **Language Requirements**: Chinese (Mandarin) B2+ for Chinese programs; IELTS 6.0+ / TOEFL 80+ for English-medium programs (IMAS, IBMBA)
- **Scholarships**:
  - Taiwan Scholarship (MOFA) — full tuition + NTD 15,000–25,000/month living allowance
  - NTU International Student Scholarship
  - NTSC (National Taiwan Scholarship for Chinese Language Students)
  - Presidential Education Award
- **Notable**: Taiwan's #1 research university. Strong in semiconductors, engineering, and biomedicine. Close to Taiwan's tech industry (TSMC, MediaTek).
- **Website**: https://www.ntu.edu.tw/english

## General Notes for Taiwan
- **Visa**: Student Visa (resident visa or visitor visa depending on length). Apply at TECO (Taipei Economic and Cultural Office) in your country.
- **Cost of Living**: Taipei: NTD 20,000–35,000/month (~$630–$1,100); Other cities: NTD 15,000–25,000/month. Taiwan is very affordable.
- **Taiwan Scholarship (MOFA)**: Highly recommended! Full tuition + NTD 15,000/month (undergrad) or 20,000/month (grad). Apply via TECO. Deadlines: Feb–March.
- **Huayu Scholarship**: For those wanting to learn Mandarin Chinese — covers tuition for language courses at any Taiwan university. Great for beginners.
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week with Alien Resident Certificate (ARC) work permit.
- **Language**: Mandarin Chinese is the official language. Taiwanese Hokkien also widely spoken. English signage is common in cities. University English proficiency varies.
- **Safety**: Taiwan is extremely safe — low crime, stable society. Excellent healthcare system.
- **Semiconductor Hub**: Taiwan dominates global semiconductor manufacturing (TSMC makes 90%+ of world's advanced chips). Exceptional for EE and computer engineering students.
