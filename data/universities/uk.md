## 1. University of Cambridge
- **QS World Rank**: #2
- **Location**: Cambridge, England
- **Type**: Public Research University (Collegiate)
- **Tuition (Undergrad — International)**: £22,227–£58,038/year (varies by program)
- **Tuition (Postgrad — International)**: £25,000–£58,000/year
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~15–21% (per college)
- **Popular Programs**: Natural Sciences, Engineering, Law, Mathematics, Economics
- **Language Requirements**: IELTS 7.5+ (most courses) / TOEFL 110+
- **Scholarships**:
  - Cambridge Trust Scholarships (merit, up to full fees + maintenance)
  - Gates Cambridge Scholarship (full funding: fees + stipend + flights)
  - Commonwealth Scholarships (Commonwealth countries)
- **Notable**: Gates Cambridge is one of world's most prestigious fully-funded scholarships. 121 Nobel laureates affiliated.
- **Website**: https://www.cam.ac.uk

## General Notes for UK
- **Visa**: Student Visa (formerly Tier 4). Need CAS from university. Processing: 3 weeks–3 months.
- **Cost of Living**: London £15,000–£22,000/year; outside London £12,000–£16,000/year
- **Part-time Work**: Up to 20 hrs/week during term on Student Visa. 2-year Graduate Route visa post-graduation (3 years for PhD).
- **Application**: Via UCAS for undergrad. Direct to university for postgrad.
- **Application Deadlines**: UCAS deadline Jan 15 (Oxford/Cambridge Oct 15); Postgrad: varies, often Jan–March
- **Scotland Note**: Scottish universities offer 4-year undergraduate degrees (1 year longer than England/Wales).
